Eligibility Criteria

1. Business Registration:

  • The applicant must be a registered business entity (Private Limited Company, LLP, Partnership, Proprietorship, etc.).
  • For individuals applying for personal projects, a valid business or project plan must be provided.

2. Age Requirement:

  • The minimum age for applicants is 21 years.
  • The maximum age at loan maturity should not exceed 65 years.

3. Project Viability:

  • A detailed project report or feasibility study must be submitted.
  • This should include project timelines, financial projections, and business strategies.
  • The project should demonstrate the ability to generate revenue or contribute to business growth.

4. Financial Stability:

  • The business must have a consistent revenue stream with a proven track record of profitability.
  • Applicants must provide audited financial statements for the past 2 to 3 years, demonstrating financial capability to repay the loan.

    • 5. Credit History:

    • Applicants must have a good credit score (generally above 650).
    • Any existing loans or liabilities must be disclosed.
    • A healthy credit history is required.

    6. Minimum Turnover:

    • The business should have an annual turnover of at least ₹50 lakhs or higher, depending on the project scope.

    7. Collateral:

    • Depending on the loan amount, collateral may be required.
    • This may include business assets, property, or equipment.

    8. Experience and Expertise:

    • The applicant or business should have prior experience in the industry or project field.
    • This helps assess the ability to manage and execute the project successfully.

    Frequently Asked Questions - FAQs

    − What is a Project Loan?

    A project loan is a type of loan specifically designed to finance large-scale projects such as infrastructure development, business expansion, real estate development, or industrial projects. These loans cover various stages of a project, from planning and implementation to completion.

    + What types of projects are eligible for a project loan?
    Project loans can be used for a variety of large-scale ventures including infrastructure development, manufacturing projects, real estate development, renewable energy projects, and business expansion initiatives.
    + How much can I borrow through a project loan?
    The loan amount depends on the project cost, financial projections, and repayment capability. Lenders typically finance up to 70%–80% of the total project cost depending on risk assessment and collateral.
    + What is the repayment tenure for project loans?
    Project loan repayment tenure generally ranges between 5 to 15 years, depending on the project size, financial structure, and lender policies.
    + Can I apply for a project loan if I am a new business?
    Yes, new businesses can apply for project loans if they provide a strong business plan, detailed feasibility report, and financial projections that demonstrate the project's potential profitability.
    + What documents are required to apply for a project loan?
    Common documents required include:
    • Business registration documents
    • Detailed project report
    • Financial statements of the business
    • Income tax returns
    • Bank statements
    • KYC documents of promoters
    + Is collateral required for a project loan?
    Is collateral required for a project loan?Yes, depending on the loan amount and lender policies, collateral such as property, machinery, or other business assets may be required to secure the project loan.
    + What interest rates apply to project loans?
    Interest rates vary based on the lender, project risk, and credit profile of the applicant. Typically, project loan interest rates range between 9% to 14% annually.
    + How long does it take to get a project loan approved?
    Project loan approvals generally take between 2 to 6 weeks, depending on project complexity, documentation verification, and lender evaluation processes.
    + Can I prepay a project loan?
    Yes, most lenders allow borrowers to prepay or foreclose a project loan before the end of the tenure. However, some banks may charge a small prepayment fee depending on the loan agreement and terms.
    + What happens if the project faces delays?
    If a project experiences delays due to unforeseen circumstances, lenders may review the situation and allow restructuring of the loan schedule. Borrowers may need to provide updated project timelines and financial projections to support the revised repayment plan.
