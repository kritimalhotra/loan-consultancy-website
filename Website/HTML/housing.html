Eligibility Criteria

    1. Age Requirement: The applicant must be between 21 and 65 years of age at the time of loan maturity. This ensures that you are in the income-earning phase of life when repaying the loan.

    2. Income Level:

    • Salaried Professionals: Must be employed with a recognized public or private sector company for at least 1 year.
    • Self-Employed and Business Owners: Must have a business vintage of at least 2 years to qualify.

    3. Employment Type:

    • Salaried Professionals: Must be employed with a recognized public or private sector company for at least 1 year.
    • Self-Employed and Business Owners: Must have a business vintage of at least 2 years to qualify.

    4. Credit Score: A minimum credit score of 650 is required to apply for a housing loan. A higher credit score improves your chances of approval and allows you to secure better interest rates.

    5. Property Location: The property must be located in India and meet all legal and construction standards, such as having the necessary permits and clear title ownership.

    6. Loan Tenure: Loan tenure can range from 10 to 30 years, depending on your repayment capacity and preference. The tenure you choose will affect your monthly EMI and the total interest paid.

    7. Down Payment: Typically, you need to make a down payment of at least 10% to 25% of the property's purchase price. The remaining amount can be financed through the loan.

    8. Financial Stability: You must demonstrate financial stability with proof of income tax returns, bank statements, and audited financials (for self-employed individuals and business owners). This helps us gauge your ability to repay the loan.

Frequently Asked Questions - FAQs

− What is the maximum loan amount I can get for a housing loan?
The maximum loan amount depends on several factors, including your income, credit score, and the value of the property. Typically, lenders finance up to 75% to 90% of the property's value. You can contact our team to determine your eligibility and loan amount.
+ What is the interest rate on a housing loan?
Interest rates depend on the bank, loan type, and your credit profile. Most housing loan rates range between 8% – 11% per year.
+ Can I get a housing loan for a property under construction?
Yes, most banks offer loans for under-construction properties, but the builder must be approved by the bank.
+ How long does it take to get a housing loan approved?
Loan approval usually takes 3–7 working days depending on document verification and bank processing time.
+ Can I prepay my housing loan?
Yes, most banks allow prepayment of housing loans with minimal or no penalty depending on the loan terms.
+ What are the tax benefits of a housing loan?
You can claim tax deductions under Sections 80C and 24(b) of the Income Tax Act for principal repayment and interest payments.
+ What documents are required for a housing loan?
Common documents include ID proof, address proof, income proof, bank statements, and property documents.
+ How is my housing loan eligibility calculated?
Eligibility depends on income, existing liabilities, credit score, age, and employment stability.
+ What is the tenure for a housing loan?
Housing loan tenure usually ranges from 10 to 30 years. The tenure depends on your repayment capacity, age, and lender policies. Longer tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest paid.
+ Can NRIs apply for a housing loan?
Yes, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) can apply for housing loans in India to purchase residential properties. Banks may require additional documentation such as overseas employment proof and NRE/NRO bank accounts.
