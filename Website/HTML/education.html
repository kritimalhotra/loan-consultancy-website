Who Can Apply for a Education Loan?

At Loan Experts, our education loans are designed to help students from all backgrounds pursue their academic dreams. Whether you're planning to study in India or abroad, our flexible eligibility criteria ensure that a wide range of applicants can apply.

Students

Any student who has secured admission to a recognized university or institution for a graduate, postgraduate, vocational, or professional course is eligible to apply. The loan can be used to cover tuition fees, accommodation, and other educational expenses.

Co-Applicants

A co-applicant (usually a parent, guardian, or spouse) with a steady source of income is required for the loan. The co-applicant acts as a guarantor and helps improve the chances of loan approval.

NRIs and International Students

Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and students planning to study abroad can also apply for an education loan to cover their tuition and living expenses at overseas universities.

Eligibility Criteria

1. Age Requirement: The applicant must be at least 18 years old at the time of applying for the loan. There is no maximum age limit for the co-applicant.

2. Admission to a Recognized Institution:

  • The student must have secured admission to a recognized institution in India or abroad.
  • Undergraduate and Postgraduate Degrees
  • Diploma Programs
  • Professional Courses (MBA, Engineering, Medicine, etc.)
  • Vocational Training Courses
  • Study Abroad Programs

3. Co-Applicant Requirement: A co-applicant is mandatory for all education loan applications. This can be a parent, guardian, or spouse.

4. Credit Score: The co-applicant should have a credit score of 650 or above.

5. Loan Tenure: Education loan repayment usually ranges from 5 to 10 years.

6. Income Criteria:

  • Co-Applicant's Income: Minimum monthly income of ₹20,000.
  • Self-Employed Co-Applicants: Must provide proof of income for the last 2 years.

7. Academic Performance: The student should meet the academic performance criteria set by the institution.

8. Debt to Income Ratio: Monthly debt payments should not exceed 50% of total income.

9. Loan-to-Value Ratio: Banks typically finance up to 60-70% of the property value.

Frequently Asked Questions - FAQs

− What expenses are covered by an education loan?

An education loan covers a wide range of education-related expenses, including:

  • Tuition fees
  • Accommodation/hostel fees
  • Books and study materials
  • Travel expenses (for study abroad)
  • Lab fees and equipment costs
  • Other related expenses such as project work or study tours
+ What is the interest rate on an education loan?
Interest rates typically range between 8% to 12% depending on the bank, loan amount, and credit profile of the co-applicant.
+ Can I apply for an education loan if I am studying abroad?
Yes. Education loans are available for students planning to study abroad in recognized universities for undergraduate, postgraduate, and professional programs.
+ What is a moratorium period in an education loan?
The moratorium period is the time during which you are not required to repay the loan. It usually includes the course duration plus 6 to 12 months after graduation.
+ Is a co-applicant mandatory for an education loan?
Yes. A co-applicant such as a parent, guardian, or spouse is usually required to guarantee the loan and ensure repayment capability.
+ How much of my education expenses can I finance through a loan?
Most lenders finance up to 90%–100% of the total education cost depending on the course, institution, and student profile.
+ What is the loan repayment tenure?
Education loan repayment tenure usually ranges between 5 to 15 years depending on the lender and loan amount.
+ Can I prepay my education loan?
Yes. Most banks allow prepayment of education loans without penalty, though policies may vary between lenders.
+ How is the education loan disbursed?
Education loan funds are usually disbursed directly to the educational institution in installments as per the fee schedule. In some cases, funds for accommodation or other expenses may be credited to the student’s bank account.
+ What happens if my co-applicant’s credit score is low?
If the co-applicant’s credit score is low, the bank may still consider the application but may ask for additional collateral, a stronger co-applicant, or may offer a lower loan amount.
+ What documents are required to apply for an education loan?
Common documents required include:
  • Identity proof (Aadhaar Card / Passport / PAN Card)
  • Address proof
  • Admission letter from the institution
  • Academic records and mark sheets
  • Income proof of co-applicant
  • Bank statements
  • Passport and visa (for study abroad loans)
